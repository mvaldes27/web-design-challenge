Latitude vs. Max Temperature

This graph shows that as latitude increases, temperature decreases. This means that temperatures tend to be warmer closer to the equator (latitude = 0 ) and cooler farther away from the equator. This is largely due to axial tilt, which leads to equatorial areas receiving more direct sunlight (and therefore more solar energy) throughout the year.
